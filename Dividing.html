Dividing Polynomials by a Monomial:

• Example 1: w^3 + 5w^2 -8w - 40/ w+5

- To solve this problem you have to solve the numerator first. To solve the polynomial you have to break it apart and factor out w^3+5w^2 and -8w-40 which factors to w^2(w+5) - 8(w+5) and you get the answer as (w^-8) (w+5). Once you get the answer you divide it by the denominator. (w^-8) (w+5)/ w+5. W+5 gets canceled out and you are left with (w^-8) which is the final answer.

A: (w^-8)

• Example 2: v^3 - v^2 - 3v + 3/ v-1)

- The same steps are repeated to solve this problem as well. First you solve the polynomial in the numerator by breaking it apart. V^3 - V^2 and -3v+3 factors out to v^2(v-1)-3(v-1) which changes to (v^2-3) (v-1) then you divide v-1 with the answer to the polynomial and you are left with v^2-3.

A: v^2-3

Dividing Polynomials by a Binomial:

- First find what multiplies to get ‘C’ and adds to get ‘B’ (ax+bx+c). Then, cancel out the common factors with the binomial you’re dividing by, and the remaining factors are your answer.

• Example 1: x^2 - 10x + 21/x - 7

Step 1: solving: :

(x - 7) (x - 3)

Now cancel out the (x-7)

Answer: x-3

• Example 2: x^2 + 8x - 33/x - 3

Step 1: solving: :

(x + 11) (x - 3)

Now cancel out the (x - 3)

Answer: x + 11

• Example 1: a^3 + 2a^2 + 9a + 18

- There are 3 steps to solve this polynomial.

#1 is grouping

#2 is factoring GCF

#3 is factoring

First you group the first 2 terms together and the last 2 terms together. In this case the first 2 terms are a^3 + 2a^2 and the last 2 are 9a + 18. Next step is to factor out the GCF from each of the binomials. A^3 + 2a^2 will be factored out to a^2(a+2) which is for the first 2 terms and 9a + 18 will be factored out to a(a+2) for the last 2 terms. The last step is to factor the common binomial out which is (a+2) so after factoring it out we get (a^2+a) (a+2).

A: (a^2+a) (a+2).

• Example 2: 10m^3 - 25m^2 + 4m - 10

First you group the first 2 terms together and the last 2 terms together. In this case the first 2 terms are 10m^3 + 25m^2 and the last 2 are 4m + 10. Next step is to factor out the GCF from each of the binomials. 10m^3 + 25m^2 will be factored out to 5m^2(2m-5) which is for the first 2 terms and 4m - 10 will be factored out to 2(2m-5) for the last 2 terms. The last step is to factor the common binomial out which is (2m-5) so after factoring it out we get (5m^2+2) (2m-5).

A: (5m^2+2) (2m-5).

Multiplying Binomials x Trinomials

- Use distributive property to multiply a polynomial by a monomial: multiply each term of the polynomial by the monomial. Multiplying coefficients and adding exponents of the appropriate variables are required. While multiplying polynomial expressions, you add the exponents.

• Example 1: 2x^2(6x^3 - 3x^2 + 5x - 3) =?

Step 1: Distributed Property:

2x^2(6x^3) + (2x^2)(- 3x2) + (2x^2)(5x) + (2x^2)(- 3)

Step 2: Solve the equation:

(2)(6)x^2+3 + (2)(- 3)x^2+2 + (2)(5)x^2+1 + (2)(- 3)x^2

Answer:

12x^5 -6x^4 +10x^3 -6x^2

• Example 2: -3a^3b(- 2a^2 + ab - 2a + 4) =?

Step 1: Distribute the Monomial:

-3a^3b(-2a^2) + (-3a^3b)(ab) + (-3a^3b)(- 2a) + (-3a^3b)(4)

Step 2: Solve and Add Exponents:

(- 3)(- 2)a^3b^1+2 + (- 3)a^3+1b^1+1 + (- 3)(- 2)a^3+1b + (- 3)(4)a^3b

Answer:

6a^3b^3 -3a^4b^2 +6a^4b - 12a^3b

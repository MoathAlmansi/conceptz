Subtracting Polynomials

- For Factoring Trinomials of the form (ax^2 + bx + c), when "a" cannot be factored out by GCF, it's still possible to factor the trinomial by using the "Slip and Slide" method. The first step of this method is to "slip" a to the end of the trinomial and multiply it by c. You then would factor the trinomial. Next, you have to divide both factors by the value you have "slipped" in the first step. Reduce fractions and "slide" any denominators up next to the variable. You can then distribute it to check if it's correct

• Example 1: 5m^2 - 17n + 6

Since there is no GCF in this equation, you would use the method "slip and slide". The first step is to multiply 6 by 5. The equation you would get is m^2 - 17m + 30. Since you can factor this trinomial, you then have to find the two integers that can be (-17) when added and (30) when multiplied. The two integers are (-15) and (-2). You would then write two binomials, which are (m - 15) (m - 2). The last step is to divide these two numbers by the number by 5. The answer is (m -3) (5m - 2). Since ⅖ is not a whole number, you would "slide" the 5 to the other side.

• Example 2: 6x^2 + x - 12

First, you multiply (6) by (-12). Next, you find the two integers and make two binomials. You would write (×+9) (x-8) . The last step is to divide both numbers by 6. The answer is (2x + 3) (3x -4)

• Example 3: 12x^2 + 2x -2

The first step is to find the GCF. For this equation, it is 2. After finding it, you would write 2(6x^2 + x-1) since you can't factor it, you multiply 1 by 6. You then write two binomials after finding the two integers. You would write 2(x+3) (-2). The last step is to divide both numbers by 6. The answer is 2(2x + 1) (x - 3). After finding the answer, you can then distribute it in order to see if it is correct.

Subtracting Polynomials

- To subtract polynomials, first, reverse the sign of each term to be subtracted (i.e., change "+" to "-" and "-" to "+"), then add as normal. To explain this easier, here are steps:

Step 1: Arrange the polynomials in their standard form.

Step 2: Place the polynomial next to each other horizontally.

Step 3: Change the signs of the second polynomial through the parentheses to its opposite sign. In other words, you're distributing the negative.

Step 4: Separate the like terms and arrange them together.

Step 5: Carry out the order of operations and solve the equation

- An Example: Subtract 2x - 3y + 6z from 5x + 8y - 12z

Step 1: Already Arranged.

Step 2: Horizontally Placed: (2x - 3y + 6z) - (5x + 8y - 12z)

Step 3: Distribute the Negative: 2x - 3y + 6z - 5x - 8y + 12z

Step 4: Arrange Like Terms: 2x - 5x - 3y - 8y + 18z

Step 5: Solve: -3x - 11y + 18z
